Day 5: 15 February 2017

Posted by Anivarth Peesapati on Wed 15 February 2017 in Current Affairs

SC convicts Sasikala

  • In a verdict for the 20 year old case, Supreme Court had convicted the former chief minister of Tamil Nadu J Jayalalitha and her aides V K Sasikala, Sudhakaran and ilavarasi for possesion of disproportionate assets.

Rule for National Anthem in cinemas changed

  • Supreme court had made a clarification to its November 30 2016, order stating that there is no need for audience to stand up when the national anthem is played as a part of the film.

Bengal's 21st district â€“ Kalimpong.

  • Chief Minister of West Bengal Ms. Mamata Banerje has announced that Kalimpong will become the 21st district of West Bengal

  • It was under the sub division of Darjeeling before the announcement.

IAF inducts Netra

  • IAF has inducted the Airborne Early Warning & Control system (AEWCS) , named Netra which was developed indigenously by DRDO.

20 meter long tunnel found

  • Border Security Force(BSF) has identified a 20m long tunnel originating from Pakistan.

4 militants & 2 soldiers killed in J & K

  • 4 militants and 2 soldiers were killed in two separate encounters in Jammu & Kashmir.

  • Officials have identified the militants as members of Lashkar-e-Taiba(LeT)

TTD estimates Rs. 2858.48 crores revenue

  • Tirumala Tirupati Devastanams(TTD) is estimating a revenue of Rs. 2858.48 crores for its financial year 2017-2018.

A lukewarm start for air show in Bengaluru

  • Claimed as the Asia's premier air show, this year there was a lukewarm response from the public as there were only few people who have attended the air show.

Trade unions oppose overhaul of Factories Act

  • Trade unions have opposed the amendment proposed to the Factories Act of 1948 . The amendment will give the state governments power to set the threshold limit.

  • They have also opposed the online registration of the factories, which will ease entrepreneurs to set up a factory without permission.

Jera buys 10% stake in ReNew

  • Japanese firm JERA had brought a 10% stake in the Indian renewable energy company ReNew .

  • Goldman Sacs, Asian Development bank, Abu Dhabi investment authority(ADIA) are other major stake holders in ReNew.

SpiceJet's profits decline 24.5%

  • Owing to increase in fuel prices and the effect of demonetization, SpiceJet's profits declined 24.5% to Rs. 181 crores in the third quarter.

BCCI financial matters to be checked by COA

  • Committee of Administrators(COA) appointed by Supreme Court will look into the financial matters of the BCCI.

Indian Players set a lower base price

  • For the upcoming 10th IPL season, Indian players have set a lower base price when compared to the previous IPL.

  • The only exception is Ishanth Sharma whose base price is Rs. 2 crores.

India makes a slow start

  • In the under 19 test match, India has made a slow start against England.

NSA chief resigns

  • National Security Advisor(NSA) to Donald Trump, Micheal Flynn had resigned on account of allegations levelled against him in regards to contacts with Russia.

Kim Jong Nam assasinated

  • North Korean leader Kim Jong-un 's half brother Kim Jong nam has been assassinated in Malaysia.
