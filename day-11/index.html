Day 11: 21 February 2017

Posted by Anivarth Peesapati on Tue 21 February 2017 in Current Affairs

Ben Stokes bought for Rs. 14.5 Crores

  • In the 10 th IPL auction, Ben Stokes was bought by Rising Pune Supergiants for a whopping Rs. 14.5 Crores.

  • Afghanistan players made their debut into IPL this time and were bought by Sunrisers Hyderabad.

Palaniswami announces a slew of schemes

  • Two days after taking over as the chief minister of Tamil Nadu, Mr. Palaniswami has introduced a slew of schemes targeting farmers and poor people.

Declaration of countries as sponsor of terrorism bill opposed

  • The D eclaration of Countries as S ponsor of Terrorism Bill, 2016 which was moved in Rajya Sabha by independent member Rajeev Chadrasekhar was opposed by the Home Ministry, as it was against Geneva conventions.

Records of Gandhi assanination to be made available

  • Central Information Commission(CIC) has directed National Archives of India(NAI) to post the records related to Gandhi's assassination on the public domain.

NPPA fixes a price cap for stents

  • National Pharmaceutical Pricing Authority(NPPA) has fixed a cap for the prices of Stents.

  • The move is to eliminate illicit brokers making the product easily accessible.

Keezhadi excavation to continue

  • Central government has given permission to continue excavation at Keezhadi after a delay of six months.

  • It is expected that Keezhadi hosted the biggest habitation of Sangam dated between 200 BC – 1000 AD

Constitution bench for Sabarimala case

  • A bench of justices has referred the sabarimala case on the issue of ban of women between the ages 10 – 50 into the temple, to a constitutional bench.

CBI books a case against its former chief

  • Central Bureau of Investigation(CBI) has booked a case against its former chief A.P. Singh on the account of graft charges.

Listing process of railway PSEs begins

  • Finance Ministry has started the process of listing the three railway PSEs – IRCTC, IRCON & IRFC through IPO.

  • The expected value though divestment is Rs. 72000 crores.

IFC invests in Granules India

  • World Bank's International Finance Corporation(IFC) has invested $47.5 million in the Hyderabad based Granules India Ltd.

TCS to buy-back 5.61 crore shares

  • TCS board had approved a buy-back of 5.61 crore equity shares accounting up to 2.5% of the total equity share capital for Rs. 16000 crores.

Google, Microsoft to demote piracy sites

  • Google and Microsoft have signed up a voluntary code of practice and they will work parallelly anti-piracy measures.

India to face SA

  • Indian women cricket team will have to face South Africe in the final of the ICC womens world cup qualifying tournment.

47 on trial for Turkey Coup

  • 47 suspects were arrested in Mugla, Turkey for plotting to assassinate Turkish President Erdogen on July 15, 2016.

Artillary increased near Afganistan Border

  • After a series of horrifying attacks, Pakistan has moved heavy artillery towards Afghanistan Border to enhance security arrangements for combating terrorism.
