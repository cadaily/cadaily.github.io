Day 8: 18 February 2017

Posted by Anivarth Peesapati on Sat 18 February 2017 in Current Affairs

Smart fence along India – Pakistan Border

  • The proposal to build a wall along the India-Pakistan border was dropped because the government was looking at more technical solutions.

  • The move is to use sensors, networks, intelligence & command to cut the costs and keep up the operational effectiveness.

National song not a fundamental duty

  • A bench of justices in supreme court had made it clear that Article 51(A) of constitution never mentioned National song as a fundamental duty.

  • The verdict was given for the centre's plans to promote Vande Mataram as National song.

ISRO's GSLV tests successful

  • ISRO chairman A. S. Kiran Kumar along with his colleagues has witnessed the successful test of GSLV MK III's cryogenic upper stage C – 25.

  • GSLVs can carry a payload of about 4 tonnes and ISRO is planning to put a communication satellite into orbit using this Launch Vehicle.

NEET mandatory for all medical exams

  • Central council of Indian Medicine has made it clear that National Eligibility – cum – Entrance Test(NEET) is compulsory for seat in any of the Ayurvedic, Yoga, Naturopathy, Unani, siddha and homeopathy streams.

Aadhaar required for Scholarships

  • Ministry of Human Resources and Development( MHRD) has made Aadhar card mandatory for the meritorious students to avail scholarships.

Minister clarifies Gen. Rawat's statements

  • Minister of State for home affairs in the Prime Minister Office(PMO) Jitendra Singh has made it clear that the Army chief General's statements were concern about the locals and not a sign of warning.

Note presses return to normalcy

  • After the centre's demonetization and remonetization drive, note presses which had worked round the clock had come to normalcy.

  • Security Printing & Minting corporation of India Ltd.( SPMCIL ), Bharatiya Reserve Bank Note Mudran Pvt. Ltd.( BRBNMPL ), Bank Note Paper Mill India Pvt. Ltd . Were employed in printing the new notes.

Ajay Tyagi gets 3 years term at SEBI

  • Ajay Tyagi who was appointed the new SEBI chairman will get a 3 year initial term at SEBI.

Banks to incur Rs. 33 Crores to reset ATMs

  • It is estimated that it would cost Rs. 33 crores for banks to recalibrate their ATM's to accommodate new currency notes.

Apple Inc. to start Assembly soon

  • Technology giant Apple Inc. is planning to assemble its low cost Iphone SE in Bangalore, India soon.

Mahindra Electric launches e20 plus

  • Mahindra Electric has launched an electric car e20 plus with an ex-showroon price in Delhi of Rs. 5.46 Lakhs.

TASL & Raytheon to made stinger air defence missile components

  • Tata Advances s y stems limited (TASL) along with Raytheon has signed a deal to make stinger defence air missile components.

TRAI to review the tariff policy

  • Telecom regulator TRAI will review the tariff policy to make it clear about the promotional offers, predatory pricing and other aspects.

Harika & Padmini make it into pre – quarterfinals

  • Indian chess players Harika & Padmini had made it into pre – quarterfinals in the women's world chess championship held at Tehran.

US – Russia relations reset will continue

  • American president Donald Trump has reiterated that his administration is committed to push for better relations with Russia.

  • Micheal Flynn NSA to Donald Trump had resigned few days back and this has created a political turmoil.
